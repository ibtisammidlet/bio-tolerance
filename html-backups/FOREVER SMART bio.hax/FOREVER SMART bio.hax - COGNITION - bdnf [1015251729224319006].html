https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3696349/ Take a look at this. We see that there is a substantial portion of the population that has defective NTRK2 which leads to a higher rate of suicide among those with depression. So of ppl with severe depression, those that had some particular variants were more likely to attempt suicide. One of these SNPs is associated with the top math class someone took. So NTRK2 variation clearly has tremendous implications for an individual's response to noots. What sorts of things might we expect to be impaired in someone with a low activity TrkB? LTP in general would probably be impaired. The glutamate system would probably tend to get dysregulated as it tries to compensate. Not having BDNF able to do its job also fucks up the amygdala and other limbic structures and tends to lead to poor mood. BDNF is largely at the core of cognitive enhancement potential. When we consider what is happening in someone's brain that produces sustained benefits after chronic TAK administration, it can be presumed that BDNF is playing a very significant role. Perhaps individuals with impaired TrkB would tend to improve less when given treatments that may slightly increase TrkB expression. As such, there is a need for alternative pathways to target to try to compensate for this inability to have normal TrkB activation in order to optimize glutamate and LTP and such.
