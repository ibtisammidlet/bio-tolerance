Pely 11/14/2023 4:54 PM
Fuck forgot to take more pictures of Lepista nuda. Found half a dozen specimens today, absolutely beautiful. Gonna make pizza with it tonight. She's on the 4th photo. Second one is the most deadly fungus on the planet... Amanita phalloides. She takes on many sizes and forms which is why people get poisoned. What you see on the photo is the typical form and color, tho' my camera doesn't show much of the green hue. Third photo is Russula atropurpurea, edible but still hot so not recommended. Brittle caps can be identified by their caps which breaks easily, no enlargement at the base and no ring on the stipe. The species with bland flavor are edible and hot ones are poisonous. Last 2 photos are Macrolepiota procera, identified by their large caps (more than 12 cm in size), snake like pattern on stipe and double rings which move easily up and down.
