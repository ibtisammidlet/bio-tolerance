Rho-associated protein kinase (ROCK) overexpression or dysregulation has been implicated in various diseases and conditions. Here are some examples: 1. Cardiovascular diseases: ROCK overexpression has been observed in conditions such as hypertension, atherosclerosis, and coronary artery disease. Increased ROCK activity can lead to vascular dysfunction, vasoconstriction, and inflammation, contributing to the development and progression of these cardiovascular disorders. 2. Pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH): In PAH, the pulmonary arteries constrict, leading to increased pulmonary vascular resistance and elevated blood pressure in the lungs. ROCK overexpression has been found in the smooth muscle cells of pulmonary arteries in PAH patients. Inhibiting ROCK activity with drugs like Fasudil has shown potential therapeutic effects in PAH. 3. Fibrotic diseases: ROCK has been implicated in fibrotic conditions, including liver fibrosis, renal fibrosis, and pulmonary fibrosis. Overexpression of ROCK contributes to the activation of fibroblasts and excessive collagen deposition, leading to tissue scarring and organ dysfunction. 4. Neurological disorders: ROCK overexpression has been associated with neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, and Huntington's disease. ROCK activation can contribute to neuronal cell death, neuroinflammation, and abnormal protein aggregation observed in these conditions.
